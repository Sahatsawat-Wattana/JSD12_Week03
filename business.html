Tiantan Publishing Co. Ltd.

Tiantan Publishing was founded in 1931 in a riverside town in southern China by Li Ming Guang, a young printer inspired by both traditional calligraphy and modern printing technology. Starting with a single manual press, the company initially produced classical Chinese texts and poetry for local scholars. Despite periods of economic and political uncertainty, Tiantan Publishing adapted by printing educational materials and expanding its reach. By the late 20th century, it had adopted digital printing methods while preserving elements of traditional craftsmanship, becoming a respected name in Chinese-language publishing known for balancing heritage with innovation.

Business Outstanding points

  • Strong blend of traditional Chinese craftsmanship and modern printing technology
  • Consistent focus on high-quality Chinese-language publications
  • Ability to adapt to market changes, from manual to digital printing
  • Reputation for reliability and cultural authenticity
  • Experience in both educational and literary publishing
  • Commitment to preserving Chinese literary heritage
